# Lay the Draw: In-Play Football Strategy

## What is Laying the Draw?

Laying the draw means **betting against the draw** on a betting exchange (like Betfair). You're saying "this match will NOT end in a draw." If either team wins, your lay bet wins. If it ends in a draw, you lose.

The real power comes from **trading in-play**: laying the draw before kick-off and cashing out after a goal goes in.

> When a goal goes in, the draw price rockets. That's where you bank your profit.

## How It Works Step by Step

### 1. Pre-Match

- Find a suitable match (more on this below)
- Lay the draw at, say, **3.20** on Betfair
- Stake: £10, liability: £22

### 2. A Goal Goes In

- The draw price jumps to, say, **5.50**
- You can now **back the draw** at 5.50 to lock in profit
- Or use the "cash out" button on the exchange

### 3. The Maths

- You laid at 3.20, backed at 5.50
- Guaranteed profit regardless of final result
- Typical profit: 40-70% of your original stake per goal

## Picking the Right Matches

This strategy doesn't work on every game. Here's what to look for:

### Green Flags

- **Both teams score frequently** — high BTTS percentage (65%+)
- **Low draw percentage** — teams that tend to win or lose, not draw
- **Home team is slight favourite** — odds around 1.80-2.50
- **Attacking styles** — avoid defensive, bus-parking teams
- **League games over cups** — more predictable patterns

### Red Flags

- **Derby matches** — too unpredictable, often cagey
- **Both teams in poor form** — can lead to 0-0 bores
- **Weather disruption** — rain can slow games down
- **Dead rubber matches** — no motivation = boring football
- **Very one-sided matches** — the underdog might park the bus

## Risk Management

### The Key Rule: Set a Stop-Loss Time

If there's no goal by the **70th minute**, consider closing the trade for a small loss. Don't let a 0-0 become a full liability hit.

### What If the Wrong Team Scores?

If the underdog scores first, the draw price might not move as much (because a comeback is expected). In this case:

- Hold if you think the favourite will equalize (draw price will then spike)
- Close for a smaller profit or scratch
- Never let emotion keep you in a trade that's gone wrong

## Expected Results

With good match selection:

- **65-70% of trades** will see a goal before 70 mins
- **Average profit per successful trade:** 50% of stake
- **Average loss per failed trade:** 100% of liability (mitigated by stop-loss)

Over 100 trades, a disciplined LTD trader can expect 15-25% ROI.
