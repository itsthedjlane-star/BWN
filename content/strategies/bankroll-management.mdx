# Bankroll Management: The Only Strategy That Matters

## Why Bankroll Management Comes First

You can be the best handicapper in the world, but without bankroll management, you'll go broke. Every sharp bettor, every professional trader, every poker player will tell you the same thing:

> Bankroll management is not a strategy. It's THE strategy. Everything else is secondary.

## Setting Up Your Bankroll

### Step 1: Define Your Bankroll

Your bankroll is the **total amount of money set aside exclusively for betting**. This is NOT your savings, rent money, or spending cash. It's money you can afford to lose.

- Recommended starting bankroll: Whatever you're comfortable losing entirely
- Common amounts: £200 - £1,000

### Step 2: Define Your Unit Size

A "unit" is your standard bet size. The standard approach:

- **1 unit = 1-2% of your bankroll**
- If your bankroll is £500, 1 unit = £5-10
- Maximum bet: 3-5 units (reserved for very high confidence picks)

### Why Small Units?

Even the best bettors hit losing runs. With 1% units:

- A 20-bet losing streak costs 20% of your bankroll
- With 5% units, that same streak wipes out your entire bankroll

Losing streaks of 10-15 bets happen to everyone. Size your units to survive them.

## The Kelly Criterion (Simplified)

The Kelly Criterion tells you the mathematically optimal bet size based on your edge:

**Kelly % = (Edge / Odds) x 100**

Where Edge = Your probability - Implied probability

### Example

- You think Arsenal has a 60% chance of winning
- Odds are 2.00 (implied 50%)
- Edge = 10%
- Kelly = (0.10 / 1.00) = 10% of bankroll

### The Problem with Full Kelly

Full Kelly is too aggressive for most people. The variance is brutal. Instead, use **Quarter Kelly** or **Half Kelly**:

- Quarter Kelly: Bet 2.5% instead of 10%
- Half Kelly: Bet 5% instead of 10%

This sacrifices some growth for dramatically reduced risk of ruin.

## Staking Plans

### Flat Staking (Recommended for Beginners)

Bet the same unit amount on every bet regardless of confidence.

- **Pros:** Simple, consistent, easy to track
- **Cons:** Doesn't capitalise on stronger edges

### Confidence-Based Staking

Vary your stake based on conviction:

- 1 star (low confidence): 0.5 units
- 2 stars: 1 unit
- 3 stars: 1.5 units
- 4 stars: 2 units
- 5 stars (max confidence): 3 units

**Warning:** Be honest with yourself. If everything is "5 stars," you're doing it wrong.

### Proportional Staking

Adjust unit size as your bankroll grows or shrinks:

- Bankroll grows to £600? Your unit goes from £5 to £6
- Bankroll drops to £400? Your unit drops to £4

This protects you during downswings and accelerates growth during heaters.

## The Rules

### Rule 1: Never Chase Losses

Lost 3 in a row? Do NOT double your next bet. This is the fastest way to blow a bankroll. Stick to your units.

### Rule 2: Never Bet More Than 5% on a Single Event

No matter how "certain" you are. Certainty doesn't exist in betting.

### Rule 3: Track Everything

Log every single bet:
- Date, sport, event, pick, odds, stake, result, P&L
- Use the BWN Bet Tracker for this

Without data, you're gambling. With data, you're investing.

### Rule 4: Review Monthly

At the end of each month, review:
- Total P&L
- ROI by sport
- Average odds of winners vs losers
- Biggest losing streak

Adjust your approach based on evidence, not feelings.

### Rule 5: Take Profits

If your bankroll doubles, withdraw your original stake. You're now playing with pure profit. The psychological difference is massive.

## Surviving the Downswings

Every bettor goes through bad patches. Here's how to survive:

- **Don't increase stakes** to "win it back faster"
- **Take a break** if you're on tilt (emotional betting)
- **Review your process**, not your results
- **Remember the sample size** — 50 bets is nothing, 500 starts to tell a story

## The Math of Ruin

With a 55% win rate at even odds (which is VERY good):

- 1% units: 0.1% chance of going broke
- 2% units: 1% chance of going broke  
- 5% units: 15% chance of going broke
- 10% units: 40% chance of going broke

The edge is real, but only if you survive long enough to realize it.

> Professional bettors don't have bigger edges than you think. They just manage their money better than you think.
