# Each-Way Racing Strategy

## What is Each-Way Betting?

An each-way bet is **two bets in one**: a win bet and a place bet. If your horse wins, both parts pay out. If it places (usually top 2-4 depending on field size), only the place part pays — at a fraction of the win odds.

> EW is the racing punter's secret weapon. It turns near-misses into paydays.

## How Place Terms Work

Place terms depend on the number of runners and the race type:

- **2-4 runners:** Win only (no EW available)
- **5-7 runners:** 1/4 odds, first 2 places
- **8-15 runners:** 1/5 odds, first 3 places
- **16+ runners (handicap):** 1/4 odds, first 4 places

### Example

You back a horse at **10/1 each-way**, £1 EW (total stake £2):

- **If it wins:** Win = £10 + Place = £2.50 = **£12.50 profit** (minus £2 stake)
- **If it places 2nd-3rd:** Place only = £2.50 = **£0.50 profit** (minus £2 stake)
- **If it finishes 4th+:** Lose both = **-£2**

## When to Bet Each-Way

### Go EW When:

- The horse is **8/1 or bigger** — at shorter odds, the place return barely covers your stake
- There are **12+ runners** — more places available, more chance of a return
- The horse has **strong place form** — consistently finishes in the first few
- It's a **handicap race** — more competitive, more chaos, more value in places
- The going suits the horse but the market hasn't adjusted

### Go Win-Only When:

- The horse is **short odds (under 4/1)** — EW returns are tiny
- There are **fewer than 8 runners** — limited places
- You have very strong conviction the horse will win

## Finding EW Value

The real edge in EW betting comes from horses whose **place probability is underpriced**:

- Consistent placers that rarely win but always run top 3
- Front-runners in big fields who may get caught but will be thereabouts
- Course specialists returning to their favourite track

### The EW Sniper Strategy

Look for horses at **12/1 to 25/1** in races with 16+ runners. At these odds with 1/4 place terms:

- A 16/1 shot placing pays **4/1 on the place part alone**
- You only need them to place roughly 1 in 5 to break even
- Any horse placing more often than that is pure profit

## Bankroll Tips for EW

- Remember your stake is doubled (£1 EW = £2 total)
- Factor this into your unit sizing
- Track win and place returns separately to find your edge
- Don't chase — if the card doesn't have value, don't bet

> The best EW punters don't find the most winners. They find the most value in the place market.
