# Value Betting: Finding Edges the Bookies Miss

## What is Value Betting?

Value betting is the cornerstone of profitable gambling. A value bet exists when the probability of an outcome is **higher** than what the bookmaker's odds suggest. In simple terms, you're getting paid more than you should be for the risk you're taking.

> If a coin flip paid 2.20 instead of 2.00, you'd flip that coin all day. That's value.

## The Expected Value Formula

**Expected Value (EV) = (Probability x Profit) - (1 - Probability) x Stake**

- If EV is positive, the bet has value
- If EV is negative, the bookie has the edge

### A Real Example

Arsenal are playing at home against a newly promoted side. You assess Arsenal's true win probability at 75%.

The bookies offer Arsenal at **1.40** (implied probability = 71.4%).

- Your probability: 75%
- Implied probability: 71.4%
- Edge: 3.6%

This is a value bet. Over hundreds of bets like this, you'll profit.

## How to Find Value

### 1. Build Your Own Odds

Before checking any bookmaker, assess the match yourself. Ask:

- What's each team's recent form?
- Are there injuries or suspensions?
- What's the head-to-head record?
- How does the venue affect things?

Assign your own probabilities, then compare to the market.

### 2. Line Shopping

**Never bet with just one bookmaker.** Odds vary significantly between books. Use odds comparison sites or the BWN odds page to find the best price.

- Bet365 might have Arsenal at 1.38
- William Hill might have them at 1.42
- Betfair might have them at 1.45

That difference compounds massively over time.

### 3. Specialize

You can't be an expert in everything. Pick 1-2 sports or leagues and know them inside out. The recreational punters bet on everything — you want to know more than them in your niche.

## Common Mistakes

- **Confusing value with winners.** A 10/1 shot that should be 8/1 is still value, even though it loses most of the time.
- **Results-oriented thinking.** A losing bet can still be a good bet if it had value. Judge the process, not the outcome.
- **Not keeping records.** Track every bet. If you can't prove your edge with data, you don't have one.

## The Long Game

Value betting isn't about getting rich quick. It's about making hundreds of +EV decisions and letting the maths work. A 5% edge means for every £100 staked, you expect to profit £5. Scale that up and stay disciplined.

> The bookies don't fear one big bet. They fear the punter who quietly takes value every single day.
